# Style Notes — *How To Read Numbers* (deeper pass)

Scope
- Based on a broader sample across the book using the Markdown conversion.
- Evidence is tied to page numbers from the PDF/Markdown. Inferences are labelled.

Voice and stance (evidence)
- Direct reader address is frequent and reassuring, especially around maths anxiety (pp. 10–13).
- The narrator positions themselves as a guide rather than an authority, often admitting uncertainty or limits (pp. 10–13, 58).
- The voice is conversational, with mild self-deprecation and light humour (pp. 10, 150).

Openings and hooks (evidence)
- Chapter openings often start with a vivid, timely example or a familiar headline-style claim, then widen into the concept (pp. 15–16, 51).
- The introduction opens with an epigraph and a human-stakes frame before moving to the technical purpose (p. 9–12).

Explanation style (evidence)
- Concepts are introduced in plain language, then clarified with concrete analogies or miniature thought experiments (pp. 30, 56, 59–60, 130).
- Explanations often include “why this matters” signposts, tying the concept to real decisions (pp. 11–12, 80, 140).
- When maths appears, it is bracketed with reassurance, and definitions are embedded in prose (pp. 13, 59–60, 130).

Use of examples (evidence)
- Examples mix public policy, health, sport, and everyday life, keeping the topic grounded (pp. 15–16, 51–56, 100–101, 150).
- Examples frequently include counter-examples or confounding variables to show limits of inference (pp. 56–58).

Pacing and structure (evidence)
- Paragraphs are short to medium, and long explanations are broken by rhetorical questions or mini-summaries (pp. 11–12, 59–60).
- The text alternates between narrative explanation and “method” sections (boxes or asides) without overloading the main flow (pp. 16, 58–60).

Rhetorical devices (evidence)
- Uses rhetorical questions to frame choices and force reader engagement (pp. 11–12, 100).
- Uses contrast pairs and “not X but Y” framing to clarify nuance (pp. 11–12, 50).
- Uses analogies from sport to convey scale and ranking differences (pp. 100–101).

Underlying aims (inference)
- The narrative is designed to be anti-dogmatic: emphasise uncertainty, nuance, and the conditions under which numbers mislead.
- The reader is treated as smart but busy; the book trades formalism for practical intuition.

Practical drafting checklist (derived guidance)
- Start each chapter with a concrete, news-adjacent example or a short anecdote.
- State the concept in one plain-English sentence, then show it in action with a second example.
- Add a “why it matters” paragraph that links back to real-world decisions.
- Use rhetorical prompts to surface ambiguity (for example: "choose the right number" and "state what it actually means").
- Keep equations minimal and explain every symbol in words.
- Include at least one counter-example or confounder per concept.
- Close with a short takeaway or rule-of-thumb.

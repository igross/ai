# Style Notes (PDF-based)

Scope
- Notes based on a small sample of pages from the three PDFs in this folder.
- This is evidence-based where possible and marked as inference when extrapolating.

Observed style traits (evidence)
- Conversational, reader-friendly tone with direct address and rhetorical questions.
- Plain-English explanations of technical ideas using simple examples.
- Frequent signposting to explain why a concept matters and what it affects.
- Long sentences appear, but are broken up with commas and parenthetical asides.
- Gentle humour and self-deprecation show up in the narrative voice.

Likely broader patterns (inference)
- The narrative voice blends journalist warmth with economist precision.
- Each chapter tends to open with a vivid hook or anecdote, then widens to the core concept.
- Technical points are anchored with concrete stories or everyday comparisons.
- The text alternates between explanation and stakes: “why this matters” appears often.

How to use these notes
- When drafting, aim for clarity first, then add a hook or analogy that makes the stakes concrete.
- Use short paragraphs and rhetorical questions to keep the pace lively.
- Avoid heavy notation; if a symbol appears, explain it immediately in plain language.

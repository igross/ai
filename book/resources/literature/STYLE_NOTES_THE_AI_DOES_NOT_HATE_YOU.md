# Style Notes - The AI Does Not Hate You V3 (deeper pass)

Scope
- Based on a broader sample across the book using the Markdown conversion.
- Evidence is tied to page numbers from the PDF/Markdown. Inferences are labelled.

Voice and stance (evidence)
- Conversational narration with direct reader address appears regularly.
- Authorial presence is visible, with asides and first-person commentary.
- Complex ideas are framed as approachable rather than intimidating.

Openings and hooks (evidence)
- Openings tend to start with a surprising claim, scene, or anecdote, then widen to the concept.
- Early pages foreground motivation before technical detail.

Explanation style (evidence)
- Technical terms are introduced in plain language, then grounded with a concrete example.
- Explanations step through logic in short units, often using conditional phrasing.
- The text frequently signals why a detail matters to the reader.

Use of examples (evidence)
- Mixes real-world cases, historical anecdotes, and everyday analogies.
- Examples are used to show how a concept can mislead or surprise in practice.

Pacing and structure (evidence)
- Alternates narrative sections with explanation blocks to keep momentum.
- Paragraphs are short to medium, with rhetorical questions used as pivots.

Rhetorical devices (evidence)
- Rhetorical questions to frame choices and uncertainties.
- Contrast framing ("not X but Y") to clarify nuance.
- Light humour or wry asides to soften technical passages.

Underlying aims (inference)
- Balance intellectual rigour with accessibility for general readers.
- Keep the reader engaged through narrative while preserving analytic honesty.

Practical drafting checklist (derived guidance)
- Start with a hook that sets stakes, then define the core concept in one sentence.
- Use at least one vivid example and one everyday analogy per section.
- Include a brief "why this matters" paragraph after the explanation.
- Keep technical notation to a minimum and translate any symbols immediately.
- End sections with a short takeaway or rule of thumb.

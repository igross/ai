# hypotheses

## h1 bundling explains apparent overpayment
When hardware is sold with warranty, imaging, security, delivery SLAs, and support, observed unit prices may exceed spot retail. Prediction: in line items where hardware and services are separable, hardware-only gaps fall materially.

## h2 reduced effective competition raises markups
Framework call-offs and direct awards can reduce search costs but may also compress bidder participation. Prediction: fewer bidders and direct-award routes are associated with larger price gaps.

## h3 governance and timing generate wedges
Compliance processes and slower award cycles can increase transaction costs and cause purchases at less favorable times relative to market declines. Prediction: longer award lags and stricter route requirements correlate with higher gaps.

## h4 threshold-induced procedure shifts affect prices
Contracts near procedural thresholds can face discrete changes in competition/transparency requirements. Prediction: price outcomes differ around threshold cutoffs if design assumptions hold.

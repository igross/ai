# notes

## framing
Hypothesis H1: triple-lock uprating acts like a politically-determined pseudo-wage for pensioners, shifting demand toward pensioner-intensive local sectors and raising their relative prices where supply is less elastic.

## key empirical tension
The same periods can feature macro shocks (energy, food, wage growth), so identification must come from cross-sectional exposure and category structure, not aggregate inflation time series alone.

## falsification mindset
If retiree-heavy places do not show stronger inflation in pensioner-intensive non-tradables around pension income shocks, the mechanism is weak.

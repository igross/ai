# propositions

## proposition 1: demand-composition relative price effect
Given `alpha_P > alpha_W`, a positive pension transfer shock increases equilibrium demand for non-tradables relative to tradables. If non-tradable supply is not perfectly elastic, the relative price `P_N/P_T` rises.

## proposition 2: exposure and elasticity heterogeneity
The effect of pension shocks on non-tradable inflation is stronger in places with higher retiree exposure and lower non-tradable supply elasticity.

## proposition 3: financing regime decomposition
Under lump-sum redistribution, observed inflation effects are primarily demand-driven. Under employer payroll-tax financing, labour-intensive sectors can show additional cost-push inflation.

## empirical corollaries
- Test interaction of pension shock with retiree share.
- Test triple interaction with supply inelasticity proxies.
- Test additional interaction with labour intensity for cost channel.

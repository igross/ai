# Tuition Fees: Concept Note (v0)

## Research question
How does incidence differ across three UK higher-education funding regimes once timing and discounting are made explicit?

Regimes:
1. Current loan-based tuition fee regime (post-2012 income-contingent loans).
2. Graduate tax regime.
3. General taxation regime.

Distributional comparisons:
- Graduates vs non-graduates.
- Households with different family wealth/income (including ability to pay fees upfront).
- PDV of payments and transfers over the lifecycle.

## Baseline model structure
Baseline choice: **simple OLG/lifecycle incidence model**.

Why OLG here:
- Transparent accounting of intertemporal payment timing.
- Direct mapping from policy rules to cohort-level incidence.
- Sufficient for speculative debate-paper goals without structural complexity.

[INFERENCE] A richer dynamic human-capital model is unnecessary at v0 because the immediate objective is comparative incidence under alternative financing rules, not schooling-demand estimation.

## Intended contribution
- Provide a clean, comparable accounting framework for who pays and when under each regime.
- Clarify how income-contingent repayment and write-offs create implicit redistribution.
- Isolate how an upfront-payment margin for wealthy households changes subsidy arithmetic and cross-subsidisation in the loan book.

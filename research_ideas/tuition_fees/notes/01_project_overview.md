# 01 Project overview

Last updated: 2026-02-25

---

## Objective

Compare incidence and welfare across three UK higher-education funding regimes in a
deliberately simple theoretical framework:

1. Post-2012 income-contingent loan regime (stylised).
2. Graduate tax regime.
3. General taxation regime.

The goal is a clean accounting comparison of who pays, how much, and when — not a structural
estimation of education demand.

## Scope

- Education choice is exogenous in the baseline model.
- The focus is on transparent PDV burden comparisons by household type.
- Institutional-policy accuracy is prioritised over model complexity.
- This is a debate paper, not a full structural exercise.

## Type dimensions for incidence reporting

- **Graduate status:** graduate ($G$) vs non-graduate ($N$).
- **Family wealth:** high ($H$) vs low ($L$) — determines ability to pay fees upfront.
- **Earnings profile:** low, median, high lifetime paths.

## Deliverables in notes phase

- Verified UK policy-rule mapping for each regime.
- Calibration targets from IFS/LEO/SLC data.
- PDV burden table and winner/loser matrix by type cell.
- Scenario sensitivity checks (discount rate, write-off horizon, earnings growth).

## Open decisions

- Baseline cohort: Plan 2 or Plan 5 as primary, with the other as robustness.
- Graduate-tax design: proportional vs thresholded, fixed-horizon vs lifetime.
- Whether to add an education-choice extension (v1) or stop at exogenous participation.
